Scenario 3 — FATCA / CRS Questions
"What is FATCA?""I don't know what to answer""What does US person mean?""I moved countries — do I need to update?"
Key rules agents must know
  • FATCA = US law; CRS = global standard. Both require banks to identify where customers pay taxes.
  • "US person" = US citizen, green card holder, or someone meeting the substantial presence test
  • If customer says yes to US person → they need US TIN (SSN or ITIN)
  • Customers MUST update declaration within 30 days if tax status changes
  • Aspora does NOT provide tax advice. Always direct to a qualified tax advisor.
Never do this:Do not tell a customer whether they are or aren't a "US person" — that's tax advice. Do not suggest answers to FATCA/CRS questions. Do not skip or dismiss FATCA concerns — it's legally mandatory.
Response template — General FATCA
Hi [Name], great question! FATCA and CRS are international regulations that require all banks globally to identify where their customers pay taxes. This helps prevent tax evasion across borders. It's not specific to Aspora — any bank you open an account with will ask the same questions. Our app makes it simple with a guided questionnaire (about 6 minutes). You just need to know which country you pay taxes in and your Tax Identification Number (TIN). If you're unsure about any answers, we'd recommend checking with a tax advisor to make sure your declaration is accurate.
Response template — US person question
A "US person" under FATCA means someone who is a US citizen, a US green card holder, or meets the IRS "substantial presence test" for US tax purposes. If you're unsure whether you qualify, we'd strongly recommend consulting a tax advisor, as this has important implications for tax reporting. We're not able to make this determination for you, but we're happy to help with any other questions about the process!

Scenario 7 — NRO to NRE Transfers
"How do I move money from NRO to NRE?""What is Form 15CA / 15CB?""My transfer got rejected"
Key rules
  • Annual limit: USD 1 million per financial year (April–March) — aggregate across ALL NRO accounts at ALL banks
  • Tax clearance is mandatory before transfer
  • If total FY transfers above ₹5 lakh: Form 15CB from CA required
Step-by-step process
  1. Customer checks remaining FY limit
  2. Gathers: passport, visa, PAN, NRO statement, source-of-funds proof
  3. Gets Form 15CB from CA (if transfers above ₹5 lakh in FY) — costs ₹500–1,500
  4. Files Form 15CA on incometax.gov.in (Part A ≤5 lakh, Part C + 15CB >5 lakh)
  5. Submits 15CA acknowledgement + 15CB + proofs to bank
  6. Bank debits NRO (principal + TDS + charges ₹250–1,000 + GST)
  7. NRE credited: same-day (intra-bank) or 1–3 working days

Scenario 10 — Tax-Related Questions
"How much tax on NRO interest?""What is DTAA?""How do I reduce TDS?""Do I need to file tax returns in India?"
Critical rule: We do NOT provide tax advice.Always recommend consulting a CA or tax advisor. You may only share factual information about how taxation works.
Factual information agents CAN share
  • NRE interest: Tax-free in India (savings and FD)
  • NRO interest: 30% TDS (+ surcharge and cess) deducted automatically
  • DTAA can reduce TDS rate (e.g., India-UK DTAA reduces interest TDS to 15%)
  • To claim DTAA: Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) + Form 10F required
  • For lower TDS upfront: apply via Form 13 on incometax.gov.in (takes 15–30 days)
  • Customer can claim TDS refund when filing Indian ITR
Agents must NOT:Tell customers whether they should file ITR · Calculate tax liability · Advise whether DTAA applies to their specific situation · Fill in or advise on Form 15CA/15CB · Interpret tax treaty provisions
Response template — TDS on NRO
Hi [Name], interest earned on NRO accounts is subject to TDS at 30% (plus applicable surcharge and cess). This is automatically deducted by the bank before the interest is credited to your account. If you're a tax resident of a country with a DTAA with India — like the UK — you may be eligible for a reduced TDS rate. To claim this, you'd need to provide a Tax Residency Certificate from your country's tax authority (e.g., HMRC for the UK) along with Form 10F. Alternatively, you can apply for a Lower TDS Certificate (via Form 13 on the Income Tax portal) to get TDS deducted at a lower rate upfront. I'd recommend consulting a Chartered Accountant for guidance specific to your situation. Let me know if there's anything else I can help with!
Response template — DTAA
Hi [Name], DTAA stands for Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement. It's a treaty between India and another country (like the UK) that prevents you from being taxed on the same income in both countries. For example, under the India-UK DTAA, the TDS rate on NRO interest income can be reduced from 30% to 15%. To claim this benefit, you'd need to submit a Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) from HMRC and a Form 10F to the bank. DTAA doesn't eliminate tax entirely — it ensures you don't pay double tax. For the specifics of how it applies to your situation, a tax advisor would be the best person to consult.

Part C — Escalation
⬆️
Escalation Do's and Don'ts
Do
  • Always include the customer's application/account ID in escalations
  • Document what you've already tried before escalating
  • Set expectations with the customer on when they'll hear back
  • Follow up on escalations if SLA is breached
Don't
  • Don't escalate without first trying to resolve at your level
  • Don't tell the customer "someone will get back to you" without giving a timeframe
  • Don't escalate KYC rejections as "discrepancies" — they are handled differently
  • Don't escalate tax advice questions — these are out of scope for all levels. Direct to a CA.
Part D — Do's and Don'ts for All Agents
✅
Always Do
  • Verify identity before sharing any account-specific information
  • Use the customer's name in responses
  • Set clear expectations on timelines ("within 24 hours" is better than "soon")
  • Log every interaction with resolution status
  • Recommend both NRE and NRO when customers ask about account types
  • Remind customers about the 30-day FATCA update obligation if they mention moving countries
  • Check for pending transactions before processing any account changes
  • Use response templates as a starting point but personalize them
🚫
Never Do
  • Never provide tax advice — no DTAA guidance, purpose codes, ITR advice, or Form 15CA/15CB. Always direct to a CA.
  • Never share internal system details (vendor names, API details, internal processes) with customers
  • Never promise specific timelines for compliance approvals
  • Never attempt to override AML/sanctions rejections
  • Never enter or handle customer passwords, PINs, or OTPs
  • Never share one customer's information with another, even if they claim to be family
  • Never tell a customer they were flagged for sanctions/AML — say "could not be approved after compliance review"
  • Never process account changes without proper identity verification
  • Never rush a compliance review even if the customer is upset
