What is Aspora?

Aspora is operated by Real Transfer Limited (RTL) — formerly known as Vance, rebranded to Aspora, backed by Sequoia Capital. RTL is an FCA-authorised payment institution (UK) for money remittance only. RTL is NOT a bank — not PRA-regulated, does not accept UK deposits. RTL acts as an introducer to partner bank Shivalik Small Finance Bank (SSFB), which holds the RBI licence and accepts deposits in India. Deposits insured by DICGC up to ₹5 lakh — UK FSCS does NOT apply, UK Ombudsman has no jurisdiction, disputes under Indian law.

Scenario 3 — FATCA / CRS Questions
"What is FATCA?""I don't know what to answer""What does US person mean?""I moved countries — do I need to update?"
Key rules agents must know
  • FATCA = US law; CRS = global standard. Both require banks to identify where customers pay taxes.
  • "US person" = US citizen, green card holder, or someone meeting the substantial presence test
  • If customer says yes to US person → they need US TIN (SSN or ITIN)
  • Customers MUST update declaration within 30 days if tax status changes
  • Aspora does NOT provide tax advice. Always direct to a qualified tax advisor.
Never do this:Do not tell a customer whether they are or aren't a "US person" — that's tax advice. Do not suggest answers to FATCA/CRS questions. Do not skip or dismiss FATCA concerns — it's legally mandatory.
Response template — General FATCA
Hi [Name], great question! FATCA and CRS are international regulations that require all banks globally to identify where their customers pay taxes. This helps prevent tax evasion across borders. It's not specific to Aspora — any bank you open an account with will ask the same questions. Our app makes it simple with a guided questionnaire (about 6 minutes). You just need to know which country you pay taxes in and your Tax Identification Number (TIN). If you're unsure about any answers, we'd recommend checking with a tax advisor to make sure your declaration is accurate.
Response template — US person question
A "US person" under FATCA means someone who is a US citizen, a US green card holder, or meets the IRS "substantial presence test" for US tax purposes. If you're unsure whether you qualify, we'd strongly recommend consulting a tax advisor, as this has important implications for tax reporting. We're not able to make this determination for you, but we're happy to help with any other questions about the process!

Scenario 7 — NRO to NRE Transfers
"How do I move money from NRO to NRE?""What is Form 15CA / 15CB?""My transfer got rejected"
Key rules
  • Annual limit: USD 1 million per financial year (April–March) — aggregate across ALL NRO accounts at ALL banks
  • Tax clearance is mandatory before transfer
  • If total FY transfers above ₹5 lakh: Form 15CB from CA required
Step-by-step process
  1. Customer checks remaining FY limit
  2. Gathers: passport, visa, PAN, NRO statement, source-of-funds proof
  3. Gets Form 15CB from CA (if transfers above ₹5 lakh in FY) — costs ₹500–1,500
  4. Files Form 15CA on incometax.gov.in (Part A ≤5 lakh, Part C + 15CB >5 lakh)
  5. Submits 15CA acknowledgement + 15CB + proofs to bank
  6. Bank debits NRO (principal + TDS + charges ₹250–1,000 + GST)
  7. NRE credited: same-day (intra-bank) or 1–3 working days

Scenario 10 — Tax-Related Questions
"How much tax on NRO interest?""What is DTAA?""How do I reduce TDS?""Do I need to file tax returns in India?"
Critical rule: We do NOT provide tax advice.Always recommend consulting a CA or tax advisor. You may only share factual information about how taxation works.
Factual information agents CAN share
  • NRE interest: Tax-free in India (savings and FD)
  • NRO interest: 30% TDS (+ surcharge and cess) deducted automatically
  • DTAA can reduce TDS rate (e.g., India-UK DTAA reduces interest TDS to 15%)
  • To claim DTAA: Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) + Form 10F required
  • For lower TDS upfront: apply via Form 13 on incometax.gov.in (takes 15–30 days)
  • Customer can claim TDS refund when filing Indian ITR
Agents must NOT:Tell customers whether they should file ITR · Calculate tax liability · Advise whether DTAA applies to their specific situation · Fill in or advise on Form 15CA/15CB · Interpret tax treaty provisions
Response template — TDS on NRO
Hi [Name], interest earned on NRO accounts is subject to TDS at 30% (plus applicable surcharge and cess). This is automatically deducted by the bank before the interest is credited to your account. If you're a tax resident of a country with a DTAA with India — like the UK — you may be eligible for a reduced TDS rate. To claim this, you'd need to provide a Tax Residency Certificate from your country's tax authority (e.g., HMRC for the UK) along with Form 10F. Alternatively, you can apply for a Lower TDS Certificate (via Form 13 on the Income Tax portal) to get TDS deducted at a lower rate upfront. I'd recommend consulting a Chartered Accountant for guidance specific to your situation. Let me know if there's anything else I can help with!
Response template — DTAA
Hi [Name], DTAA stands for Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement. It's a treaty between India and another country (like the UK) that prevents you from being taxed on the same income in both countries. For example, under the India-UK DTAA, the TDS rate on NRO interest income can be reduced from 30% to 15%. To claim this benefit, you'd need to submit a Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) from HMRC and a Form 10F to the bank. DTAA doesn't eliminate tax entirely — it ensures you don't pay double tax. For the specifics of how it applies to your situation, a tax advisor would be the best person to consult.

Part C — Escalation
⬆️
Escalation Do's and Don'ts
Do
  • Always include the customer's application/account ID in escalations
  • Document what you've already tried before escalating
  • Set expectations with the customer on when they'll hear back
  • Follow up on escalations if SLA is breached
Don't
  • Don't escalate without first trying to resolve at your level
  • Don't tell the customer "someone will get back to you" without giving a timeframe
  • Don't escalate KYC rejections as "discrepancies" — they are handled differently
  • Don't escalate tax advice questions — these are out of scope for all levels. Direct to a CA.
Part D — Do's and Don'ts for All Agents
✅
Always Do
  • Verify identity before sharing any account-specific information
  • Use the customer's name in responses
  • Set clear expectations on timelines ("within 24 hours" is better than "soon")
  • Log every interaction with resolution status
  • Recommend both NRE and NRO when customers ask about account types
  • Remind customers about the 30-day FATCA update obligation if they mention moving countries
  • Check for pending transactions before processing any account changes
  • Use response templates as a starting point but personalize them
🚫
Never Do
  • Never provide tax advice — no DTAA guidance, purpose codes, ITR advice, or Form 15CA/15CB. Always direct to a CA.
  • Never share internal system details (vendor names, API details, internal processes) with customers
  • Never promise specific timelines for compliance approvals
  • Never attempt to override AML/sanctions rejections
  • Never enter or handle customer passwords, PINs, or OTPs
  • Never share one customer's information with another, even if they claim to be family
  • Never tell a customer they were flagged for sanctions/AML — say "could not be approved after compliance review"
  • Never process account changes without proper identity verification
  • Never rush a compliance review even if the customer is upset

2
Document Validity — Expired or Unacceptable Address Proof (POA)
"Utility bill should not be older than 3 months""Broadband bill cannot be accepted""Bank statement must include at least one customer-initiated transaction"
Root Cause

POA is either expired (>3 months), lacks required transaction activity, or is not an accepted document type.

Accepted POA Types
Utility bill (electricity, water, gas, property tax receipt) or Bank statement — all dated within last 3 months and notarized.

PBM call opening · principles · scenario scripts · tone guidelines
Objective of the Opening
🎯
The first 10–15 seconds should achieve
  1. Establish legitimacy quickly
  2. Sound operational/support-oriented rather than sales-oriented
  3. Reduce resistance and perceived effort
  4. Create continuity ("your application / your process")
  5. Position the PBM as a helpful single point of contact
  6. Avoid giving away control too early
  7. Create familiarity naturally without testing memory
What to Avoid
✗
Weak Opening 1 — Asking Permission First
"Hi, this is Pratiksha Das from Aspora NRI Banking, is it the right time to talk?"
Why this is weak
  • Sounds like a generic sales call
  • Triggers an automatic "I'm busy" response
  • Asks for permission before establishing value
  • Does not explain the purpose of the call
  • Creates uncertainty and friction immediately

"I hope you remember, we connected while you were opening the account."
Why this is weak
  • Places cognitive burden on the customer — "Do I remember? Should I remember?"
  • Creates subtle social pressure
  • Sounds uncertain and validation-seeking
  • Weakens confidence and authority
The PBM should establish continuity naturally — not ask the customer to confirm familiarity.

Owner: Product · Audience: Product Managers + Engineering · Status: Draft
✏️ Editing mode — click any text to edit
📋 1. Overview

This document describes how a customer's KYC case moves through our CRM after they finish digital onboarding on the app. It covers what the bank's KYC agents see, the queues a case can sit in, and what makes a case move from one queue to the next — all the way from a frozen new account to an active, fully usable one.

It is written for two audiences. Product managers should be able to read it end-to-end and understand the flow. Developers should be able to build the queue routing, tagging, status changes, and agent actions from it without needing a separate spec.

This document deliberately avoids API contracts, data models, schemas, and rollout plans. Those will be handled separately by engineering.

📖 2. Glossary

A quick reference for the terms used throughout this doc.

TermPlain-English meaning
CIFCustomer Information File. The unique customer ID the bank creates when onboarding starts. One CIF can hold multiple accounts (e.g., NRE + NRO).
NRE / NRO accountsThe two account types we offer to NRI customers. The KYC workflow treats both the same.
Total freezeThe account exists but cannot receive money or send money. The starting state for every new account.
Credit freezeThe account cannot receive money. Lifted when KYC is approved so the user can fund the account.
Debit freezeThe account cannot send money out. Stays on after KYC approval and is lifted only after both initial funding and address are verified.
KYC agentA bank employee who reviews onboarding cases inside the CRM and takes the approve / reject / discrepant decision.
Initial fundingThe first deposit the customer makes into their new account after KYC is approved.
Welcome letterA physical letter sent to the customer's communication address. Its successful delivery verifies the address.
QueueA view inside the CRM that groups cases needing the same kind of attention. Cases move between queues as their state changes.
CaseThe unit of work in the CRM. One case = one customer's KYC journey from submission to final state.
🗺 3. How a case moves end-to-end
3.1 The starting point
  1. The customer finishes onboarding on the app and submits all required information and documents.
  2. The system allocates a CIF and account numbers for the customer.
  3. Both accounts open in total freeze — money cannot move in or out.
  4. The submitted information flows to the bank, a case is created in the CRM, and the case lands in the KYC Open Queue.
3.2 The agent's decision

A KYC agent picks the case from the KYC Open Queue, reviews the customer's information and documents, and chooses one of three outcomes:

  • Approve — KYC looks good.
  • Mark Discrepant — there is an issue and the customer needs to fix or supply more information.
  • Reject — the case fails AML, has a serious KYC lapse, or breaks a bank rule. The customer cannot have an account.
3.3 Branch A — Approved

When the agent approves a case, three things happen in parallel:

  1. The accounts move from total freeze to debit freeze. The credit freeze is lifted, so the customer can now fund the account.
  2. A welcome letter is triggered to the customer's communication address to verify the address is real.
  3. The case moves from KYC Open Queue to Debit Freeze Queue.

From here, two things still need to happen before the account is fully usable: initial funding and address verification via letter delivery. These can happen in either order.

Sub-flows inside the Debit Freeze Queue
• If initial funding lands first → case moves to Address Verification Pending Queue
• If address is verified first (via letter delivery callback) → case moves to Initial Funding Queue
Address verification is callback-driven
When the welcome letter is delivered, the courier sends a delivery callback to the CRM and the address-verification status updates automatically. No agent action needed for this step.
Final approval (manual)
Once both initial funding and address verification are complete, the agent picks the case manually, performs two separate verifications (funding + address), then manually removes the debit freeze. The case moves to Active Users Queue.

⚠️ The agent only removes the debit freeze after both verifications are done. If only one is done, the case waits.
3.4 Branch B — Discrepant

If the agent finds an issue with the KYC (missing doc, mismatched info, unclear photo, etc.), they mark the case as Discrepant. The case moves to the Discrepant Queue and the customer is asked to submit additional information.

What the customer doesOutcome
Resubmits in time — info is satisfactoryCase re-enters KYC Open Queue with Re-submission tag → follows normal Approved flow (§3.3)
Resubmits in time — info is still unsatisfactoryCase stays in Discrepant Queue. Agent can request more info again.
Does not submit within X days (configurable)Case moves to Lapsed Queue → CIF + accounts closed, KYC marked invalid, customer notified. Fresh onboarding required.
Why the Re-submission tag matters
It tells the reviewing agent that this case has history. They can pull up previous discrepancy notes and decide faster, instead of treating it like a brand-new submission.
3.5 Branch C — Rejected

If the customer's KYC fails AML, has a serious KYC lapse, or breaks a bank rule, the agent rejects the case.

  • The case moves to the Rejected Queue and stays there permanently.
  • The customer cannot open an account with us, ever, on this profile.
  • Rejected is final — there is no path back to Open or Approved.

⚠️ 7. Edge cases & rules to build for
  • Re-submission tag. When a discrepant case re-enters KYC Open Queue, it must carry a visible Re-submission tag. The agent UI should make this clearly distinct from brand-new cases.
  • Configurable lapse window. The number of days (X) before a discrepant case lapses must be a backend configuration an authorised non-engineer can change. No code release needed to update it.
  • Either-order completion. Initial funding and address verification can complete in any order. Routing must handle both paths cleanly without forcing a sequence.
  • Address verification is callback-driven. The CRM listens for a delivery callback from the courier and updates address-verification status automatically. The agent only verifies it as part of the final 2-step check.
  • Two separate manual verification steps. "Verify funding" and "Verify address" are two distinct actions with two distinct audit trails — not collapsed into a single click.
  • Debit freeze removal is gated. The "Remove debit freeze" action must only be enabled once both verifications are marked complete. If only one is done, the action stays disabled.
  • Final states are sticky. Active, Rejected, and Lapsed are all final-state queues. No transitions out of them.
  • Lapsed = full reset. When a case lapses, the CIF and accounts close and the KYC is marked invalid. The customer is notified. The only path back is fresh digital onboarding — new CIF, new accounts, new KYC.
  • Welcome letter is fire-and-watch. Triggered the moment KYC is approved; the system then waits for the delivery callback. See Open Questions for the undeliverable scenario.
